The function of a protein is largely a result of its primary sequence, but not all positions in the protein sequence are equally important for determining function. Some positions are absolutely critical for protein function, while others are less important.

Evolutionary theory suggests that functionally critical residues should be highly conserved across all members of a protein family. Residues responsible for functional differences in protein subfamilies may have more restricted distributions of evolutionary conservation.

Our INTREPID algorithm (Sankararaman and Sjölander, Bioinformatics 2008) exploits patterns of evolutionary conservation across a protein family phylogeny to infer functionally critical residues for a query sequence.

The INTREPID server has been designed to be easy to use: paste a standard FASTA-formatted protein query sequence in the text box, then click the "Submit" button.

Although easy to use, the INTREPID server performs a number of complex analyses, including retrieving homologous proteins, constructing a multiple sequence alignment, inferring a protein family phylogeny, building a comparative 3D structural model of the query sequence, and identifying evolutionarily conserved residues.

Due to the computational complexity of these analyses, results may not be available for an hour or more. In the case of extremely large protein families with thousands of highly divergent homologs, analyses can take a few hours to complete.

After submitting your sequence for analysis, a URL link will be provided, which will allow you to check the progress of your analysis at a later time. You may also provide your email address, in which case an email will be sent to you when your results are ready.
